Composting is the natural process of turning organic waste, such as leaves and food scraps, into a beneficial fertilizer that can improve soil and plants. Composting is the decomposition process of creating the perfect habitat or environment for bacteria, fungi, and other decomposing creatures to carry out their functions. There are also many benefits composting can give the environment like reducing waste fills, cutting methane emissions, improving soil health, lessening erosion, conserves water, reducing food waste, and many more. This is why composting is a beneficial way of planting for our environment because it has so many good benefits and keeps plants healthier. Composting is a process in which organic waste is turned in to something that can greatly benefit the plants without the use of money.

SIP Setup Documentation

SIP Project Title:
The Effect of Organic Compost to the number of tomatoes produced by the Solanum Lycopersicum (Tomato) Plant.

Problem:
Does organic compost affect the fruits produced by the Solanum Lycopersicum (Tomato) plant?

Hypothesis:
If the Solanum Lycopersicum (Tomato) plant is planted in the organic compost, then it will surely grow more tomatoes.

Variables:

Independent Variable:
Type of compost used to plant the Solanum Lycopersicum (tomato) plant.

Dependent Variable/s
The number of tomatoes produced.

Constant or Controlled Variables:
  • Amount of Water
  • Type of soil
  • Full sunlight exposure
  • Size of pot

TREATMENTS / SET-UP :

Control Setup:

  • In the controlled set-up, the member will grow three Solanum lycopersicum (tomato) plants but this time they won’t be using the organic compost to plant the tomato plant.

  • What will be used/seen: Medium sized pots, full sunlight exposure, 50ml/1-2 inches worth of water, same type of soil, same type of plant, same location of plant

  • No. of Trials or Replicates: 3

    Experimental Setup:

  • In the experimental set-up, the member will grow six Solanum Lycopersicum (tomato) plants (to be separated into two groups of three) but as it is done, they will plant it in the organic compost used.

  • What will be used/seen: Medium sized pots, full sunlight exposure, 50ml/1-2 inches worth of water, same type of soil, same type of plant, same location of plant, same type of organic compost used.

  • No. of Trials or Replicates: 3


EXPERIMENTAL DATA to be MEASURED or OBSERVED :

Experimental Measurement or Observation
  • Height Of Plant
  • Growth Rate Of The Plant
  • Fruits Produced of the Plant

Description of Measurement or Observation:
  • Measurement Of How Big The Plant Is
  • Measurement Of How Fast The Plant Grows
  • Measurement of how many fruits produced of the Plant

    • Significance of Observation:
  • Measures The Growth Rate Of The Plant
  • It Will Show The Impact Of the Compost On the Plant’s Growth
  • It will show the impact of the compost of how many fruits will be produced.

      • Procedures for Testing and Gathering/Obtaining Data

      Producing or Sourcing the Compost Material (Include: preparation of starting materials; a descriptive process flowchart, or composting set-up diagram or illustration, if needed; time period of composting.):

      • Prepare the materials soil, compost material (eggshells & banana peels), pots, water (50 ml for each pot)

      • Prepare the soil and the compost material and mix the required amount with the soil

      • Then get the pots and fill with the required amount of soil compost mix

      • Firm the soil to remove air pockets then poke holes for the tomato seeds (holes must be a distance apart)

      • Place a seed in each hole then add soil to cover the holes once again gently firm the mix to remove air pockets (do this process for the 3 pots)

      • Then water each pot with 50 milliliters of water/ 1-2 inches worth of water

      • Replicate the process for the other 3 pots but without the compost and only using normal soil

      • Place the pots in a area with sunlight exposure

      • Wait for the seeds to sprout (5 to 10 days)


        • Sourcing and Preparing the Test Plants and Plant Soil:

      • Record the day the compost was made and when the seeds were planted

      • The seeds will sprout within 5 to 10 days

      • Observe and measure the differences within the 2 sets and record these measurements
        (also include the date of the observation)
